### Avoid the two-beat move

Say it once.

- No colon where the right side restates or intensifies the left. If the clause after the colon is the content, delete the clause before it.
- No "not X, Y." No "X, and it's more than X." No "X — actually Y." Decide which one is true and open with that.
- No comma splices for texture.
- No "is just" definitions unless you'd defend the simplification under challenge.
- No metaphor dropped in as if it were shared vocabulary. Either it's standard or you flag it as yours.
- No meta-frame before the content ("quick status before the design question," "the load-bearing thing here"). Start at the content.
- No closing reversal that reframes what you just said.

Check: if a sentence's shape implies you weighed a simpler version and rejected it, either show that comparison or drop the implication.
